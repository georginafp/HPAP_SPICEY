Background

Single-cell RNA and ATAC sequencing allow high-resolution profiling of gene expression and chromatin accessibility, revealing cell-type-specific regulatory programs. Yet, tools to quantify specificity across multiple cell types are still limited (Lim et al., 2024).

SPICEY (SPecificity Index for Coding and Epigenetic activitY) is the first R package to quantify cell-type specificity from single-cell multi-omic data, offering an intuitive pipeline for analyzing and visualizing transcriptomic and epigenomic specificity.

Methods

Figure 1: SPICEY workflow. Differential single-cell RNA/ATAC data and region–gene annotations are used to compute specificity and entropy metrics, enabling the construction of tissue-specific regulatory networks.

Cell type specificity indices

  • RETSI (Regulatory Element Tissue Specificity Index): quantifies cell-type specificity of regulatory regions.
  • GETSI (Gene Expression Tissue Specificity Index): measures cell-type specificity of genes.

Both metrics are based on weighted specificity scores that quantify how enriched each feature (gene or regulatory region) is within individual cell types, with higher values indicating greater tissue specificity:

\[ TSI_{i,x} = \frac{FC_{i,x}}{\max(FC)_x} \cdot w_{i,x} \]

\[ w_{i,x} = \text{rescale}\left( -\log_{10}(padj_{i,x}),\ [0, 1] \right) \]

where:

  • \(i\) = cell type
  • \(x \in \{r, g\}\), with \(r\) = regulatory region; \(g\) = gene
  • \(\text{FC}_{i,x}\) = fold-change of feature \(x\) in cell type \(i\)

Entropy-based specificity Indices

Measure the distributional skewness of feature activity/expression across cell types:

\[ H = - \sum_{i=1}^{N} p_i \log_2(p_i) \]

\[ H_{norm} = 1 - e^{H} \] where:

  • \(i\) = cell type
  • \(p_{i,x} = \frac{a_{i,x}}{\sum_{j=1}^{N} a_{j,x}}\) = activity/expression of feature \(x\) across \(N\) cell types
  • \(H_{norm}\) = normalized shannon entropy [0,1].

Feature activity or expression is normalized across cell types to form a probability distribution, where low entropy indicates specificity to a few cell types, and high entropy reflects broad, uniform activity.

Region-to-gene annotation

To help construct tissue-specific gene-regulatory networks, SPICEY optionally annotates regulatory regions to their putative target genes using (1) nearest-gene (based on proximity to the closest TSS), or (2) co-accessibility (using correlated chromatin accessibility).

Results

SPICEY reveals cell-type specific regulatory activity in human pancreatic islets

SPICEY scores from non-diabetic human pancreatic islets (HPAP) (Authors, 2022);(Kaestner et al., 2019);(Fasolino et al., 2022) showed distinct specificity patterns across cell types, highlighting the heterogeneous regulatory and transcriptional landscape of the pancreatic islets (Figure 2).

Figure 2: Distribution values reflect higher specificity in tissue-specific features (brown) compared to ubiquitous (green)

Known tissue-specific promoters and enhancers have higher specificity than ubiquitous ones. Thus, SPICEY captures how cell-type-specific gene expression aligns with cell-specific cis-regulatory activity. (Figure 3).

Figure 3: Promoter and distal regions show higher SPICEY scores for tissue-specific genes.

Known ubiquitous sites have higher entropy -indicating widespread activity- while tissue-specific sites have significantly lower entropy -reflecting a bias towards cell-type-specific activity- (Figure 4), confirming that SPICEY effectively captures regulatory and transcriptional specificity in human pancreatic islets.

Figure 4: Entropy metrics reveal lower values for tissue-specific features, confirming stronger cell-type specificity.


SPICEY uncovers both known and novel markers in human pancreatic islets

SPICEY identified well-known markers in endocrine cells (INS, IGF2, SST, IRX1/IRX2) (Hauge-Evans et al., 2009) as well as less common ones like ADCYAP1, SIX2, and LRFN5 (Yamamoto et al., 2003);(Bevacqua et al., 2021); (Elgamal et al., 2023).

It recovered established markers such as REG1A/B, PI3, KRT family, and COL6A1, for non-endocrine cells while also capturing novel potential candidates like GIMAP genes in endothelial cells (Figure 5).

Figure 5: Top SPICEY cell-type-specific features. The heatmap displays the top 5 genes for each cell type based on RETSI and GETSI z-scores. Rows represent genes and columns represent cell types. Darker colors indicate higher specificity.

Conclusions

  • SPICEY is an R package that quantifies cell-type specificity from single-cell multi-omic data using combined differential and entropy-based metrics, ideal for studying complex tissues using common single-cell workflows.

  • Identifies both, known and novel markers across well-defined and less-studied cell types, distinguishing tissue-specific from broadly active features.

  • Annotates regulatory elements to target genes helping elucidate tissue-specific regulatory networks.
